Giovanna Cacciola
Vocalist for Uzeda. Vocals on the Rachel's Selenography album
Bill Callahan
The creative force behind that crazy Smog thing.
Carey Calvanese
Nina Canal
Was in the noisey NYC band UT
Heather Cantrell
Louisvillian, guitarist in the now-defunct Drinking Woman (Link?), which Tara Jane O'neill was also in. Wife of ex-Slint and The For Carnation founder Brian McMahon.
Brendan Canty
Drummer for Fugazi, contributed to the All Scars "project." Also played in Rites of Spring, One Last Wish, Deadline and Happy Go Licky. Brother of James Canty. Played with his brother, and other relative: Siobhan Canty on Siobhan's solo project.
James Canty
Guitarist and keyboardist for The Make*Up and once drummer for the Nation of Ulysses. Has also contributed to the All Scars project. Brother of Brendan Canty. Played with his brother, and Siobhan Canty on Siobhan's solo project.
Paul Caporino
In Chestnut Station (Link?) and M.O.T.O.
Cath Carroll
Musician. Wife of Santiago Durango (Link?). From Britain, her music is influenced heavily by Brazilian dance. Mark Robinson from Unrest has an obsession with her. He had a portrait of her made for an Unrest record, and even has a song named after her. (http://centerstage.net/chicago/music/whoswho/CathCarroll.html)
Brooks Carter
Musician, artist, poet, and more. (http://www.carterbrooks.com/carter/)
Laura Carter
Has worked with Buzz Hungry. Sang for the Barbeque Killers (Link?) and Jack O Nuts (Link?).
Jason Castaldi
Jay Castaldi is in Lynard's Innards and is the Mail Order supervisor at Touch and Go.
Chad Castetter
Guitar on Metroschifter's first three albums. Guitar in Endpoint. Had a band called Wino that broke up in May 99. Wino has a CD and several singles for sale on the Baltimore label Temporary Residence.
Jerome Charles
Mike Chartrand
Bassist for Big'N.
Neil Chastain
Has played drums with Thee Screamin' Meemies. Founder of the performance art/music group SAFMOD. Worked with Steev Hise in an electroacoustic improv group called Ears Under Siege (Link?). Original drummer for Craw and recorded on first two albums. Currently in Pureplex.
Damon Che
Drummer for Don Caballero and guitarist for Thee Speaking Canaries. Has also done some bass playing for Chavez.
Ken Chojnacki
Gunther Christochowitz
DouDou Christophe
Manager of the French band Sloy.
Rene Christophe
Manager of the French band Sloy.
J. Christopher
Vocals in Riflesport (Link?).
Maria Christopher
Played guitar and sang for the Dirt Merchants.
Sue Clarke
Liz Clayton
Publisher of Wind-Up Toy zine. Originated the Chicago Shows List.
Andy Cohen
Guitarist for Silkworm.
Eric Cohen
Tench Coxe
Guitarist for Bedhead.
Chris Colbourn
Plays bass, guitar, cello and vocals in Buffalo Tom
Todd Cole
Drummer for the Didjits. Contributed to Fred Schneider's solo album "Just Fred."
Kim Coletta
Ex-Jawbox, helps in the running of De Soto Records.
Byron Coley
Co-owns K'EY Records with Thurston Moore. Has contributed to Forced Exposure magazine. Big lover of noise.
Stacy Conde
Capitol Records marketing director, promotes(ed) The Jesus Lizard (Link?) at Capitol and Touch and Go.
Clint Conley
Played bass for Mission of Burma and has worked with Yo La Tengo.
Anna Connolly
Brian Connolly
Was the guitarist for the Canadian instrumental gods Shadowy Men On A Shadowy Planet.
Cynthia Connolly
Photographer. Known for her photographs of Washington, D.C. musicians with their cars. (See: http://www.southern.com/southern/band/CYNTH/)
Andee Connors
Drummer for A Minor Forest and P.E.E. (Link?) Does web design for Aquarius Records.
Steve Connors
Tony Conrad
Tony Conrad was a member of LaMonte Young's Dream Syndicate in the early-mid 1960's along with John Cale, and also found the paperback copy of The Velvet Underground from which the band took its name. Has worked with Faust. Major contributor to the early experimental/ambient forces.
Sarah Contorer
Guitarist for The Smoothies. Currently in The Prescriptions (Link?).
Pete Conway
Usually known as Flour (Link?). Bassist in Breaking Circus and Riflesport (Link?). Solo artist on Touch and Go. Contributed to a Pigface album or two. Also a carpenter, he helped to build Electrical Audio.
Wilie Conway
Jon Cook
Bass/voc in Cerebellum (Link?) 1988-1989. Bass/voc in Crain 1989-1994. His latest projects have been Experimental Pollen, who have a self-released single out and a split LP with Castetter's Wino on Louisville's Ghetto Defendant label.
Todd Cook
Plays bass at live shows for Retsin. Also is from Crain. He was also the drummer for Rodan on and off, such as on the "Aviary" cassette. He also played in Truckstop, a one-time supergroup consisting of Rodan plus ex-Ruby Falls/Retsin superstar Cynthia Nelson for the movie "Half-Cocked". It's got tracks by Retsin, Rodan, Truckstop (Link?), and Crain.
Ben Corrigan
Daniel Corrigan
Photographer, has photographed Shellac, among others.
Chris Cosgrove
Ex-drummer for Zoom (Link?) (Lawrence, KS) and played on the last dis- (Link?) record.
Gerard Cosloy
Member of improv duo Air Traffic Controllers (Link?) and founder of L.
Kevin Coultas
Drummer in Rodan 1992-1994. Drums in Sonora Pine and Rachel's.
Kevin Coutts
Brian Cowsley
Liz Cox
Former drummer for Christmas, before she became Miss Lily Banquette, the chanteuse in Combustible Edison.
Troy Cox
Bassist in Louisville band Evergreen (Link?).
Sheila Cronin
Sound Engineer at Sub Pop's studios
James Crump
Photographer for the Chicago Tribune.
Michael Cudahy
Ex of Christmas. Currently goes as "The Millionaire" in Combustible Edison.
