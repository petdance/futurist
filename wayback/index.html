Shellac
The Futurist

The Futurist is also referred to as the "Friends Of Shellac" record. Apparently, the boys did an album of music for some sort of dance production, and decided to press it to vinyl and give it to 779 of their closest friends.

The sleeve is black with silver printing. The entire front of the sleeve is printed with the names of each of the recipients of the record. It looks a lot like the cover of XTC's Go 2. The differing colors in the scan at right is because we had to graft two separate scans together. The full-sized scan is 252K.

The band apparently wants the record to not get into the hands of anyone besides the original recipient. Each receipient's record has their name circled in silver ink (although you [intentionally] can't see it on our scan). This is so that if any of them turn up for sale, the band will know whose copy it is.

We here at Action Park happened along a copy of the album, and compiled a list of the friends of Shellac, as listed on the front cover, and provided commentary as we were able. Please understand that this is a work in progress. If you have updates or corrections, please send them in.

I'd like to thank the following people for helping out on this little project: Sarah Stierch, Gareth, Stephen E Schiller, Koichi Terasawa, DJ Fatkid, Josh and Nate.

The following review of the Futurist and Terraform can be found at http://english.hss.cmu.edu/bs/reviews/1998-07-30-2.51PM.html.
